# Lighting Track Curriculum

## 3-Day Intensive | 15-30 Participants

### Overview

Lighting is where art meets engineering. A great lighting designer doesn't just make things bright. They shape emotion, direct attention, and transform a stage into something that moves people. But before you can design, you need to understand the tools and the craft.

This track gives you the technical foundation to work on professional lighting crews. You'll learn to program on grandMA3, rig and focus fixtures, build and run cue stacks, and understand the infrastructure that makes modern lighting systems work. This is the same knowledge base that every lighting professional builds on, whether they end up as a programmer, a technician, or a designer.

### What You'll Learn

**Day 1: Fundamentals**

How light works. Color temperature, beam angle, intensity, color mixing. These aren't abstract concepts. They're the vocabulary you'll use every day to communicate with your team and execute a design.

Fixture types and applications. Moving heads, wash lights, spots, beams, strobes, blinders, LEDs, conventionals. Each tool has a purpose. You'll learn when to use what and why certain fixtures show up on every rider while others are specialty items.

DMX and control basics. Every modern lighting system runs on DMX. You'll understand addressing, universes, data distribution, and the signal chain from console to fixture. When something doesn't work, you need to know how to trace the problem.

**Day 2: Programming**

grandMA3 console workflow. This is the industry standard for touring and large-scale production. You'll learn the interface, patching, fixture selection, attribute control, and the logic that makes MA powerful.

Building cues and sequences. Programming isn't just about recording looks. It's about building a show that can be executed reliably night after night. You'll learn cue structure, timing, effects, and how to organize a showfile that makes sense.

Busking and live operation. Not every show is fully programmed. You'll learn to run lights in real time, responding to music and moments as they happen. This is where instinct meets preparation.

**Day 3: Production**

Rigging and focusing. Before the console comes out, fixtures need to be in the air and pointed in the right direction. You'll participate in a full load-in, hanging fixtures, running data and power, and focusing a rig.

Previz and documentation. Modern lighting workflows start long before the truck arrives. You'll get an introduction to previsualization tools and learn how to read and create lighting plots, hookups, and channel schedules.

Show run. You'll program and operate a live performance, taking a design from concept to execution. This is where everything comes together.

### Equipment

Participants will train on:
- grandMA3 consoles (full size and light)
- Robe, Martin, and Elation moving fixtures
- ETC and Chauvet LED fixtures
- MA 3D and Depence for visualization
- Professional truss and rigging hardware

### Prerequisites

No prior lighting experience required. We start from the fundamentals and build up. You should be comfortable with technology, able to follow technical instruction, and ready to work at height if needed.

### Career Pathways

Graduates of this track are prepared for entry-level positions as:
- Lighting technician
- Follow spot operator
- Programmer assistant
- Electrician / Spark
- Lighting crew chief

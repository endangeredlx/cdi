# Audio Track Curriculum

## 3-Day Intensive | 15-30 Participants

### Overview

This track is for people who want to work in live sound. Not bedroom producers. Not studio engineers. Live sound is its own discipline, and it demands a specific skillset: the ability to think fast, troubleshoot faster, and deliver consistent results in high-pressure environments where there's no "fix it in post."

You'll learn to mix front of house and monitors on industry-standard consoles. You'll understand system design, signal flow, and RF coordination. And you'll do it all with your hands on real gear, under real conditions.

### What You'll Learn

**Day 1: Foundations**

Signal flow from source to speaker. This is where most people get lost, and it's where we start. You'll trace audio signals through the entire chain: microphones, stage boxes, snakes, consoles, processing, amplification, and loudspeakers.

Console fundamentals on digital platforms. We work primarily on DiGiCo and Yamaha systems because that's what you'll encounter on professional tours and festivals. You'll learn channel strips, routing, aux sends, and how to build a mix that translates.

Input patching and gain structure. Get this wrong and nothing else matters. We'll cover proper gain staging, headroom management, and why your input list is the most important document on the gig.

**Day 2: System Work**

PA system design and deployment. Flying line arrays, ground stacking subs, delay towers. You'll learn the physics behind why we place speakers where we do and how to achieve consistent coverage across a venue.

System tuning and optimization. Introduction to measurement tools and how engineers use them to make informed decisions. This isn't about memorizing EQ curves. It's about understanding what you're hearing and knowing how to fix it.

RF coordination and wireless management. Modern shows run dozens of wireless channels simultaneously. You'll learn frequency coordination, antenna placement, and how to avoid the disasters that happen when RF goes wrong.

**Day 3: Show Operations**

Monitor mixing for artists. This is where trust gets built. Artists depend on their monitor engineer to deliver exactly what they need to perform. You'll learn how to read an artist's needs, build monitor mixes quickly, and make adjustments on the fly.

Changeover procedures and festival workflow. When you have 15 minutes between acts, there's no room for confusion. You'll practice efficient changeovers, proper labeling systems, and the communication protocols that keep a festival running.

Live mixing session. You'll mix a live performance start to finish, applying everything you've learned in a real-world scenario with real artists and real stakes.

### Equipment

Participants will train on:
- DiGiCo SD-Series consoles
- Yamaha CL/PM series consoles
- L-Acoustics and d&b audiotechnik speaker systems
- Shure Axient Digital wireless systems
- Smaart measurement software

### Prerequisites

Basic understanding of audio concepts is helpful but not required. What we do require is a willingness to learn, the ability to take direction, and the physical capability to handle the demands of live production work.

### Career Pathways

Graduates of this track are prepared for entry-level positions as:
- Audio technician / A2
- Monitor tech
- System tech
- RF coordinator
- Audio crew chief

---

*This is a hands-on program. You will be on your feet, moving gear, and working with equipment for the duration of the training.*

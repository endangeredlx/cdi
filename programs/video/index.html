Specialized Track

Video Track

Video has transformed live entertainment. What used to be a stage with some lights is now an immersive visual environment with LED walls, projection mapping, IMAG, and real time content.

Duration 3 Day Intensive
Class Size 15 to 30 Participants
Level Entry to Intermediate
Overview

The Demand for Video Professionals Has Exploded

The skillset required goes far beyond knowing how to press play. This track covers the core competencies for working in live event video: LED systems, media servers, camera operation, and signal distribution.

You'll learn how to build, operate, and troubleshoot the video systems that power modern productions.

Curriculum

What You'll Learn

Three days of intensive, hands on training covering every aspect of live event video production.

Day 1

Display Technology

  • LED Wall Systems Different pixel pitches, panel types, and processing methods. Learn how LED walls are built, hung, and configured.
  • Signal Flow and Processing Trace the path through switchers, scalers, processors. Understanding your signal chain is critical to troubleshooting.
  • Resolution and Refresh Rates Learn to read a signal, understand genlock, and why 59.94 and 60 are not the same thing.
Day 2

Content and Servers

  • Media Server Fundamentals Resolume, Disguise, Notch. Learn basic server operation, content playback, and console integration.
  • Content Preparation Receive, check, and prepare content for playback. Codec considerations, resolution, and output mapping.
  • Show Control Integration How video systems integrate with lighting, audio, and automation through timecode, MIDI, and network protocols.
Day 3

Camera and IMAG

  • Camera Operation IMAG puts the artist on the big screen. Learn basic camera operation, framing, and live performance discipline.
  • Switching and Directing Understanding what the director needs. See the switching side and learn how a multicam show comes together.
  • System Deployment Participate in a full video system load in and load out, understanding workflow and physical demands.
Equipment

Professional grade LED wall systems for touring and installations

Brompton LED Processors

Premium video processing for high end LED displays

Novastar LED Processors

Widely deployed LED control and processing systems

Resolume Arena

Industry leading media server software for live events

Blackmagic ATEM Switchers

Professional broadcast video switchers for live production

Prerequisites

What You Need

Comfort with computers and technology is essential. Prior video experience is not required, but you should be someone who can learn software quickly and troubleshoot logically. Video work involves both creative and technical thinking.

Video technology evolves constantly. CDI's curriculum reflects current industry practice and is updated regularly to keep pace with new systems and workflows.

Career Pathways

Where This Leads

Graduates of this track are prepared for entry level positions as:

  • LED Technician
  • Video Playback Operator
  • Camera Operator
  • Video Engineer Assistant
  • Media Server Technician

# Rigging Track Curriculum

## 3-Day Intensive | 15-30 Participants

### Overview

Everything in the air is rigging's responsibility. The PA, the lights, the video walls, the scenic elements. Rigging is the discipline that makes it all possible, and it's the one where mistakes have the most serious consequences.

This track is about safety first and always. You'll learn the fundamentals of theatrical and arena rigging, understand load calculations, and develop the hands-on skills to work safely at height. This isn't a certification course. It's a foundation that prepares you to learn on the job under the supervision of experienced riggers.

### What You'll Learn

**Day 1: Principles and Safety**

Physics of rigging. Forces, loads, and how they distribute through a rigging system. You don't need to be an engineer, but you need to understand why we do what we do. This knowledge keeps you and everyone else safe.

Hardware identification. Shackles, spansets, steels, motors, chain hoists, truss, bridles. You'll learn to identify rigging hardware, understand rated capacities, and know what to inspect before every use.

Safety protocols and inspection. Every piece of rigging hardware has a working load limit. You'll learn to read ratings, perform visual inspections, and understand when something needs to come out of service.

**Day 2: Systems and Calculations**

Bridle calculations. When loads don't hang straight down, the math matters. You'll learn basic bridle angle calculations and understand why rigging points need to be rated for more than just the load they're holding.

Motor control systems. Chain hoists don't move themselves. You'll learn motor control basics, chain motor operation, and the safety protocols for flying loads in and out.

Truss systems. Ground support, flown systems, and how they go together. You'll work with industry-standard truss, learning assembly, connection points, and load distribution.

**Day 3: Practical Application**

Work at height. If you're going to be a rigger, you need to be comfortable in a harness. You'll learn proper harness fitting, lanyard use, and safe work practices for elevated positions.

Ground rigging operations. Not all rigging happens in the air. You'll learn ground support assembly, ballast calculations, and the coordination required between ground crew and fly crew.

Load-in simulation. You'll participate in a complete rigging load-in, applying everything you've learned in a realistic production scenario with proper supervision and safety protocols.

### Equipment

Participants will train on:
- CM Lodestar and Prolyft chain motors
- Prolyte and Tyler truss systems
- Spansets, steels, and rigging hardware
- Fall protection equipment
- Motor control systems

### Prerequisites

Physical fitness is required. Rigging work is demanding. You'll be lifting, climbing, and working at height. You should have no fear of heights and be comfortable with physical labor. Prior experience is not required, but this track is not for everyone.

### Career Pathways

Graduates of this track are prepared for entry-level positions as:
- Stagehand (rigging focus)
- Ground rigger
- Fly crew
- Rigging assistant

Advanced rigging positions (head rigger, arena rigger) require additional training, certifications, and years of experience. This track is the starting point, not the destination.

---

*Safety is not negotiable. CDI's rigging program emphasizes proper procedure and risk awareness at every stage. Participants who cannot follow safety protocols will be removed from the training.*

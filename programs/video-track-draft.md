# Video Track Curriculum

## 3-Day Intensive | 15-30 Participants

### Overview

Video has transformed live entertainment. What used to be a stage with some lights is now an immersive visual environment with LED walls, projection mapping, IMAG, and real-time content. The demand for video professionals has exploded, and the skillset required goes far beyond knowing how to press play.

This track covers the core competencies for working in live event video: LED systems, media servers, camera operation, and signal distribution. You'll learn how to build, operate, and troubleshoot the video systems that power modern productions.

### What You'll Learn

**Day 1: Display Technology**

LED wall systems. Different pixel pitches, panel types, and processing methods. You'll learn how LED walls are built, hung, and configured. You'll understand why a 2.8mm panel looks different from a 4.8mm panel and when each is the right choice.

Signal flow and processing. Video signals need to get from source to screen, and a lot happens in between. You'll trace the path through switchers, scalers, processors, and learn why understanding your signal chain is critical to troubleshooting.

Resolution, refresh rates, and format conversion. These technical specifications matter. You'll learn to read a signal, understand genlock, and know why 59.94 and 60 are not the same thing.

**Day 2: Content and Servers**

Media server fundamentals. Resolume, Disguise, Notch. These platforms power the content on modern tours. You'll learn basic server operation, content playback, and how servers communicate with lighting consoles and timecode systems.

Content preparation and formatting. The best content in the world won't help if it's in the wrong format or resolution. You'll learn how to receive, check, and prepare content for playback, including codec considerations and output mapping.

Show control integration. Video rarely operates in isolation. You'll learn how video systems integrate with lighting, audio, and automation through timecode, MIDI, and network protocols.

**Day 3: Camera and IMAG**

Camera operation for live events. IMAG (image magnification) puts the artist on the big screen. You'll learn basic camera operation, framing, and the discipline required to shoot live performances where there are no second takes.

Switching and directing basics. Understanding what the director needs helps you be a better camera operator. You'll see the switching side of IMAG and learn how a multicam show comes together.

System deployment and strike. You'll participate in a full video system load-in and load-out, understanding the workflow and physical demands of getting a video system up and running.

### Equipment

Participants will train on:
- ROE Visual and Absen LED panels
- Brompton and Novastar LED processors
- Resolume Arena media servers
- Blackmagic ATEM switchers
- Professional broadcast cameras

### Prerequisites

Comfort with computers and technology is essential. Prior video experience is not required, but you should be someone who can learn software quickly and troubleshoot logically. Video work involves both creative and technical thinking.

### Career Pathways

Graduates of this track are prepared for entry-level positions as:
- LED technician
- Video playback operator
- Camera operator
- Video engineer assistant
- Media server technician

# The Opportunity

> When a global artist performs across Africa, the lights, sound, and video walls are often operated by crews flown in from abroad. Local technicians—eager, capable, but untrained in international standards—watch from the wings. CDI exists to change that equation.

**Africa's stages are ready to shine—but they need trained crews.**

| Stat | Impact |
|------|--------|
| 85% | of African live events rely on imported technical crews |
| $2B+ | annual production spending leaves the continent |
| $5B+ | projected African music industry value by 2026 |
| 0 | formal production training programs in most West African markets |

---

# What CDI Does

A comprehensive ecosystem for technical production training

## 📚 Learning Arm
School programs and hands-on training for local technicians in lighting, audio, video, and rigging. University partnerships across three continents.

## 🎯 Technical Advisory
On-site and virtual production management. Industry professionals mentoring and supporting local crews on real productions.

## 🎬 Production Arm
Direct production support during transition, phasing out as local teams become self-sufficient. Building capacity for world-class events.
